# Domain: Teaching & Training

**Fits:** teachers, professors, corporate trainers, workshop facilitators, coaches, tutors — anyone whose performance is measured by whether *other people* walk out changed.
**One performance =** one class, session, workshop, or coaching block.
**"Landed" means:** observable learning — learners could do or explain the thing, asked questions that built on the material, stayed engaged without being managed into it.

## Context Fields (Phase 0)

- **Learner level** (beginners, mixed, advanced, reluctant/mandatory attendance)
- **Group size + setting** (1:1, seminar, classroom, virtual, workshop)
- **Material** (first delivery, repeat, revised — and what changed)

## The Five Dimensions

| Slot | 1 looks like | 5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **The Open** | Dove into content cold; learners didn't know why they were there | Hooked them, activated what they already knew, made the "why this matters" land before content started |
| **The Thread** | A coverage march — got through the material | Concepts built on each other toward one objective; learners could feel the staircase |
| **Command** | One explanation, repeated louder when it failed; examples improvised badly | Multiple routes to every key concept, examples ready, checked understanding before moving on |
| **The Turn** | Lost the room to confusion or disruption and pushed on anyway | Read the room, pivoted, turned the confusion itself into the lesson |
| **The Close** | The clock ended the session mid-thought | Learners demonstrated the skill, knew what's next, and the last two minutes sealed the objective |

## Phase 1 Phrasing

- Highlight: "What's the moment it clicked for someone — what were you doing, and how did you know it clicked?"
- Stall: "Where did you lose them — glazed looks, side chatter, the same confusion twice? What were you explaining?"
- Surprise: "What question or reaction did you not see coming?"

## Drilling Table Additions

| They say | Ask this |
|---|---|
| "They got it" | "How do you know — what did a learner *do* that proved it?" |
| "It was a tough group" | "Tough at which moment specifically — and was it the group, or the material's fault at that point?" |
| "Ran out of time again" | "Which section ate the time — and is it worth what it costs?" |

## Friction Categories

Persistent confusion on a specific concept, disengagement, challenges to your authority or the material's relevance, mixed-level tension (bored fast learners + lost slow ones), disruption. Verdict = did your handling restore learning, hold steady, or lose more of the room?

## The Close, Specifically

If learners left without demonstrating anything — no exit question, no attempt, no summary in their own words — you don't actually know if it landed, and Close scores 3 or below on principle. "I covered it" is not "they caught it."
