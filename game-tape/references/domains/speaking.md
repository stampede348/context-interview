# Domain: Talks & Speaking

**Fits:** conference talks, keynotes, webinars, panels, internal all-hands presentations, lectures to large audiences, comedy and performance speaking.
**One performance =** one talk / session in front of an audience.
**"Landed" means:** the audience gave energy back — laughter or reactions at the intended beats, questions that engaged with your actual argument, people quoting your lines back to you afterward.

## Context Fields (Phase 0)

- **Audience type + size** (practitioners, execs, mixed, general public; 20 / 200 / 2,000)
- **Format** (keynote, breakout, webinar, panel, internal)
- **Material** (new talk, repeat, revised — and which revision)

## The Five Dimensions

| Slot | 1 looks like | 5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **The Open** | Started with logistics, an apology, or an agenda slide | Hooked the room inside 60 seconds — they knew why this mattered before they knew your name |
| **The Thread** | Slide-by-slide information dump; sections interchangeable | One spine; every section visibly earned its place and built toward the ending |
| **Command** | Read the slides, monotone, pacing off, ran over time | Owned the room — pauses landed, slides served you, hit time to the minute |
| **The Turn** | Rattled by Q&A, a heckle, or a tech failure; rambled or froze | Crisp answers, bridged hostile questions back to the argument, turned disruptions into moments |
| **The Close** | "So... yeah, that's it, thanks" | An ending people wrote down — clear final line, clear ask or takeaway |

## Phase 1 Phrasing

- Highlight: "Which moment got the reaction you wanted — which slide or line, and what did the room do?"
- Stall: "Where did the room go quiet in the wrong way — phones, shuffling, glazed eyes? What section were you in?"
- Surprise: "What reaction, question, or comment did you not see coming?"

## Drilling Table Additions

| They say | Ask this |
|---|---|
| "Good energy in the room" | "Name the specific beat that proved it — which line, which slide?" |
| "The middle dragged a bit" | "Which slide were you on when you felt it drag — and what would you cut?" |
| "Q&A was fine" | "What was the hardest question, verbatim if you can — and are you happy with your answer?" |

## Friction Categories

Hostile or off-topic Q&A, tech failures, a flat room, panel colleagues eating your time, the expert in row two. Verdict = did your handling win the room back, leave it neutral, or lose more ground?

## The Close, Specifically

Endings get remembered; middles get forgiven. If the final 60 seconds were improvised or trailed off, score Close 2 or below even if the rest soared — and the One Thing probably lives here.
