# Domain: Negotiation

**Fits:** deal negotiations, procurement and vendor conversations, salary and offer negotiations, contract terms, partnership discussions — any conversation where the outcome is agreed terms.
**One performance =** one negotiation session (a multi-session negotiation gets one entry per session, linked by codename).
**"Landed" means:** the exchange moved toward your planned target — a concession you extracted, a frame they accepted, pressure you absorbed without giving ground.

## Context Fields (Phase 0)

- **Counterparty type** (procurement, founder, hiring manager, opposing counsel, vendor...)
- **Stakes band** (rough size/importance — no real figures if sensitive)
- **Relationship** (one-shot vs. ongoing) and **session #** in this negotiation

## The Five Dimensions

| Slot | 1 looks like | 5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **The Open** | Reacted to their frame and their agenda from minute one | Set the anchor, the agenda, and the tone before they did |
| **The Thread** | Concessions with no pattern; drifted toward "split the difference" | Every concession traded for something, on a planned path toward your target |
| **Command** | Surprised by their facts; fuzzy on your own numbers, alternatives, walk-away | Numbers, BATNA, and walk-away point all live in the room — deployed, not recited |
| **The Turn** | Filled silence with concessions; folded or flared under an ultimatum | Named the tactic, held the silence, countered from prepared ground |
| **The Close** | "Let's keep talking" with nothing locked — or agreed to terms you regret | Terms locked and confirmed, or a clean walk-away with the door held open on your terms |

## Phase 1 Phrasing

- Highlight: "What's the exchange you'd study on film — what did you say, and what did it get you?"
- Stall: "Where did momentum turn against you — what had just been said?"
- Surprise: "What did they reveal — a priority, a constraint, a fear — that wasn't in your prep?"

## Drilling Table Additions

| They say | Ask this |
|---|---|
| "We made progress" | "Name one thing you got and one thing you gave. Which was bigger?" |
| "They played hardball" | "What was the exact pressure move — and what did you do in the ten seconds after it?" |
| "We're close" | "Close on whose terms? What's still open, and who blinked last?" |

## Friction Categories

Anchoring and extreme first offers, ultimatums and deadlines, strategic silence, good-cop/bad-cop, authority games ("I'd have to check with..."), nibbling after agreement. Verdict = did your counter hold your position, cost you nothing, or cost you ground?

## The Close, Specifically

Negotiations die in ambiguity. If the session ended without written-down agreed points and explicit open items, score Close 3 or below — "I think we agreed" is where deals unravel. A confident, well-prepared walk-away is a 5, not a failure.
