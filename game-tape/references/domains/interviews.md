# Domain: Job Interviews

**Fits:** candidates at any level, any field — screens, panels, technical rounds, final rounds. (An interviewer improving their own interviewing can flip the lens; the engine works the same.)
**One performance =** one interview conversation.
**"Landed" means:** the interviewer engaged with your answer — followed up with genuine curiosity, took notes at the right moments, sold *you* on the role, got specific about next steps.

## Context Fields (Phase 0)

- **Company type/stage** (startup, enterprise, agency, public sector...)
- **Role + round** (screen, hiring manager, panel, technical, final)
- **Interviewer persona** (recruiter, future manager, peer, skip-level, bar-raiser)

## The Five Dimensions

| Slot | 1 looks like | 5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **The Open** | Rambled through "tell me about yourself," no shape | Crisp positioning in 90 seconds that framed the whole conversation |
| **The Thread** | Disconnected answers; interviewer had to assemble the story themselves | Every answer reinforced the same 2–3 themes you chose in advance |
| **Command** | Rambling answers, buried the result, no numbers, wrong length | Structured answers (situation → action → result), concrete outcomes, stopped talking at the right time |
| **The Turn** | Defensive or tap-danced on gaps, "why are you leaving," or a stumper | Direct and honest, reframed to strength, recovered fast from what you didn't know |
| **The Close** | "No, I think you covered everything" | Questions that flipped the dynamic + a clear read on process, timeline, and their hesitations |

## Phase 1 Phrasing

- Highlight: "Which answer would you keep exactly as delivered — and what did the interviewer do when you gave it?"
- Stall: "Where did their energy drop — checking the clock, cutting you off, moving on fast? Which answer were you in?"
- Surprise: "What did they ask or focus on that you didn't prepare for?"

## Drilling Table Additions

| They say | Ask this |
|---|---|
| "I think it went fine" | "Which answer would you re-record if you could? That's the one we file." |
| "They seemed to like me" | "What did they say in the last five minutes — exact words if you can?" |
| "The technical part was rough" | "Which specific question — and did you say 'I don't know' cleanly or try to bluff it?" |

## Friction Categories

Gap probes (employment gaps, short stints, "why leaving"), skepticism about fit or seniority, technical stumpers, curveball/behavioral pressure questions, compensation probes. Verdict = did your response satisfy them and move the conversation forward, leave them neutral, or visibly raise a flag?

## The Close, Specifically

A strong close is two-way: you asked at least one question that made *them* think, and you left knowing the timeline and their biggest hesitation about you. If you don't know their hesitation, score Close 3 or below — you can't address what you never surfaced.
