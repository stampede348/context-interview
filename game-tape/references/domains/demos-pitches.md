# Domain: Demos & Pitches

**Fits:** presales / sales engineers, account executives running product demos, founders pitching investors or customers, anyone selling with a screen or a deck in front of a decision audience.
**One performance =** one demo, pitch, or presentation meeting.
**"Landed" means:** visible buy-in in the room — leaning in, "can it also...", talking about themselves using your product, agreeing to a concrete next step.

## Context Fields (Phase 0)

- **Vertical/industry** (generic is fine — "discrete mfg," "SaaS," "healthcare")
- **Audience personas** (ops, IT, finance, exec, technical evaluators — and roughly how many)
- **Stage** (first demo, deep-dive, competitive bake-off, final round)

## The Five Dimensions

| Slot | 1 looks like | 5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **The Open** | Launched into the product cold | Room aligned on *their* problem before the first screen |
| **The Thread** | Feature tour; clicks without a why | Every section tied to a pain they told you about |
| **Command** | Lost in navigation, backtracking, dead screens, demo-data surprises | Clean paths; the product looked easy because you made it look easy |
| **The Turn** | Rattled by objections, tap-danced, or over-answered | Acknowledged, answered or parked cleanly, moved on |
| **The Close** | "Send me something" / no next step | Specific next step, date, and owner secured in the room |

## Phase 1 Phrasing

- Highlight: "What's the one moment you'd put in a highlight reel — what was on screen, and what did they do?"
- Stall: "Where did you lose the room, even for a minute — phones out, side conversations, silence? What were you showing?"
- Surprise: "What did they ask about or care about that wasn't in your plan?"

## Drilling Table Additions

| They say | Ask this |
|---|---|
| "They loved the product" | "Which screen got the reaction — and who reacted?" |
| "Standard demo, nothing special" | "Which section, if you cut it, would nobody in that room have missed?" |
| "The technical people were happy" | "What did the *decision-maker* do while the technical people were happy?" |

## Friction Categories

Objections (price, fit, "our current system does that", competitor comparisons), skeptical hard questions, "can it do X" traps, stakeholder crossfire in the room. Verdict = did your response visibly move them, leave them flat, or push them back?

## The Close, Specifically

A demo close is a calendar event, not a feeling. If the meeting ended without a specific next step + date + owner, score Close 3 or below regardless of how warm the room felt.
